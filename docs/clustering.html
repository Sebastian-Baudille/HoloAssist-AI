Approach

Why DBSCAN over K-Means and AprilTag

The project went through three approaches to cube detection:

MethodErrorExact-countLimitation
AprilTag 3 (base HoloAssist)~5–10 mm100%Physical markers on every cube face. Not our code.
K-Means (initial baseline)2.65 cm~90%Requires knowing cube count k upfront. Sensitive to init.
DBSCAN (current)1.63 cm82% *Merges cubes at <2× spacing. Fix in progress.

* 82% on original 1.5× spacing dataset. Expected >90% after regenerating dataset at 2.0× spacing.

DBSCAN wins on the two metrics that matter: better accuracy than K-Means, and no need to know how many cubes are in the scene. This makes it usable in the real deployment scenario where cube count is unknown.

Validation

Dataset & evaluation methodology

Guy built a fully automated dataset capture pipeline (dataset_capture.py) that runs inside the Gazebo simulation and captures 60 scenes with 2–4 cubes per scene at random positions. Ground-truth cube poses are read directly from Gazebo’s physics state at capture time — no estimation.

Evaluation: Hungarian matching

verify_detection.py uses Hungarian matching (scipy linear_sum_assignment) to optimally pair detected centroids to ground-truth cube positions, then reports mean centroid error, exact-count rate, recall, and false-positive rate separately.

The spacing bug & fix

The 82% exact-count rate was caused by a scene generation bug: cubes were placed with a minimum separation of 1.5× cube size, giving only ~2 cm surface gaps. DBSCAN with eps=0.015 m bridges this gap when depth noise fills it, merging two adjacent cubes into one cluster.

Fix: minimum cube spacing increased to 2.0× (4 cm surface gap). The fix has been committed; the dataset is being regenerated to confirm the expected >90% exact-count rate.

# Regenerate dataset with fixed spacing ros2 run holoassist_sim dataset_capture.py --scenes 60 --min-separation 2.0 # Benchmark results python clustering/verify_detection.py --ply ~/holoassist_pointclouds/ --labels dataset/labels/

Results

Results & limitations

Successes

  • 1.63 cm centroid accuracy is within 3.3× sensor noise floor (σ=0.005 m) — strong given that depth noise is the fundamental limit
  • 100% cube recall: every cube detected in every validated scene
  • No physical markers or prior cube count needed — generalises to any number of objects in the height band
  • XYZ-only clustering is robust to Gazebo lighting variation where RGB-weighted clustering failed

Limitations

  • Close-range merging: cubes <2× cube size apart merge into one cluster. Dataset spacing fix in progress.
  • Fixed geometry: height crop assumes known table height and cube size. A dynamic scene would need plane-fitting.
  • Simulation only: validated in Gazebo, not yet confirmed on the real D435i sensor.

Impact on RL training

The centroid output (world-frame XYZ, shape (12,) for 4 cubes) feeds directly into the PPO observation vector. The 1.63 cm accuracy is well within the robot’s end-effector positioning tolerance, meaning perception error is not the bottleneck in policy performance.
