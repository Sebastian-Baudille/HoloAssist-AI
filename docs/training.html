Policy

PPO Policy Design

The reinforcement learning agent uses RSL-RL PPO via Isaac Lab's DirectRLEnv API to learn reach and pick-and-place behaviours for the UR3e arm with an OnRobot RG2 gripper. Training runs on NVIDIA Isaac Sim 5.1 with 4096 GPU-parallel environments on an RTX 4070 Ti SUPER.

Environment Design

The reach task uses Isaac Lab's modular strategy pattern with separate modules for observations, actions, and rewards:

  • Observations: ground-truth 12-D (joint positions + end-effector + target)
  • Actions: joint-delta control scaled to joint limits
  • Rewards: dense distance-based with convergence bonus and action penalty

Action Space

Joint-delta actions across 6 joints, scaled per-joint to respect limits. DirectRLEnv handles clipping and physics stepping.

Reward Shaping

Multi-phase reward guides the policy through the full pick-and-place sequence:

PhaseSignalDescription
ReachDenseDistance to target cube
GraspSparseBonus on successful grasp
TransportDenseDistance to bin while holding
PlaceSparseBonus on successful place
PenaltiesDenseCollision, time, joint limits

Training

Training Setup

GPU-Parallel Training

4096 environments run in parallel on a single GPU. A 500-iteration run takes ~20 minutes wall-clock on an RTX 4070 Ti SUPER — roughly 100× faster than CPU-based Gazebo training.

Training Results (500 iters)

  • Reward: -100 → -20 (monotonic improvement)
  • Episode length: stabilises at ~110/200 steps
  • Reach success: ~60-70% of the time
  • Status: partially converged, needs ~1000-1500 more iterations + entropy tuning

Commands

# Train (from IsaacLab directory on Windows) isaaclab.bat -p scripts\reinforcement_learning\rsl_rl\train.py ^ --task HoloAssist-Reach-Direct-v0 --num_envs 4096 --max_iterations 1500 # Evaluate trained policy isaaclab.bat -p scripts\reinforcement_learning\rsl_rl\play.py ^ --task HoloAssist-Reach-Direct-v0 --num_envs 32 # Monitor with TensorBoard tensorboard --logdir IsaacLab/logs/rsl_rl/holoassist_reach_direct/
